# Brand/Enterprise Market Analysis: Creator Management Tools

**Research Date:** March 3, 2026  
**Focus:** Enterprise creator management platforms and market opportunities

---

## Executive Summary

The influencer/creator management platform market is experiencing explosive growth, projected to expand from **$25.44B (2024)** to **$97.55B by 2030** at a 23.3% CAGR. Despite this growth, significant gaps exist in serving mid-market brands, multi-creator management workflows, and affordable analytics solutions. Current enterprise solutions are prohibitively expensive ($2,000-$35,000+/year), leaving SMBs and agencies underserved.

**Key Insight:** Most platforms solve either "social media scheduling" OR "influencer discovery/vetting" — few excel at **end-to-end multi-creator campaign management with real-time analytics aggregation across platforms**.

## 1. Enterprise Social Tools Landscape

### A. Social Media Management Platforms

#### **Sprout Social**
- **Positioning:** Enterprise-grade social media management + analytics
- **Pricing:** $199-$399/seat/month (Standard to Advanced)
  - Standard: $199/mo, 5 profiles, 1 user
  - Professional: $299/mo, unlimited profiles, 1 user
  - Advanced: $399/mo, adds team reporting, chatbots, AI assist
  - Enterprise: Custom pricing
- **Key Features:**
  - Unified social inbox (cross-platform)
  - Advanced analytics and reporting
  - Team collaboration & approval workflows
  - AI-powered suggestions (Professional+)
  - Social listening (Enterprise only)
- **Pain Points:**
  - **High cost:** $199/mo minimum, each user = full seat price
  - **No true multi-creator management:** Built for brand-owned accounts, not external creators
  - **Limited influencer features:** No creator discovery, outreach, or payment tracking
  - **Poor support:** 24/5 only, slow response times reported

#### **Hootsuite**
- **Pricing:** Similar to Sprout ($99-$249/mo starting range based on profiles)
- **Strengths:** Broad platform coverage, established brand
- **Weaknesses:** Dated UX, limited analytics vs. Sprout, no native influencer tools

#### **Later**
- **Focus:** Visual-first scheduling (Instagram-heavy)
- **Pricing:** $6/mo and up per channel
- **Gap:** Very limited analytics, no team collaboration on lower tiers, no influencer management

#### **Buffer**
- **Pricing:** Free plan available; paid from $6/mo per channel
- **Positioning:** Simple, affordable scheduling
- **Gap:** Minimal analytics, no influencer workflow tools, limited team features

**Market Insight:** These tools dominate **brand-owned social account management** but **fail at managing external creators at scale**. They're built for "posting content we create" not "managing 50 influencers creating content for us."

### B. Influencer Management Platforms

#### **Modash**
- **Positioning:** Developer-focused influencer data API + platform
- **Pricing:**
  - Platform: $299/mo starting
  - **Discovery API:** $16,200/year (3,000 credits/mo)
  - **Raw API:** $10,000/year (40,000 requests/mo)
- **Database:** 350M+ creators (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube)
- **Key Features:**
  - AI-powered influencer search
  - Fake follower detection
  - Audience demographics & engagement analytics
  - Real-time content monitoring (Raw API)
- **Use Case:** Best for **data-heavy applications** and developers building custom tools
- **Gap:** Platform itself requires technical setup; not a "plug-and-play" workflow tool

#### **Phyllo**
- **Positioning:** Unified social media API aggregator
- **Key Value:** Single SDK to access Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, Twitter
- **Features:**
  - OAuth-based data access (compliant)
  - Normalized data formats across platforms
  - Influencer vetting & social screening
  - Real-time sentiment tracking
- **Pricing:** Not publicly disclosed (likely enterprise/API-based)
- **Strength:** Solves **API aggregation hell** — one integration vs. 5+ platform APIs
- **Gap:** Technical product; requires dev team to build UI/workflows on top

#### **Grin**
- **Positioning:** All-in-one creator management for e-commerce brands
- **Pricing:** ~$20,000/year (~$1,667/mo minimum)
- **Key Features:**
  - Influencer discovery with advanced filters
  - Email outreach automation
  - Product seeding (Shopify integration)
  - Influencer analytics & auditing
  - Payment & contract management
  - Content library & rights management
  - Affiliate link/discount code generation
- **Target:** E-commerce DTC brands managing 50-500 creators
- **Strength:** **Deep e-commerce integration** — product seeding is seamless
- **Gap:** High price locks out SMBs; requires Shopify/e-commerce setup

#### **AspireIQ (Aspire)**
- **Pricing:** ~$2,299/mo (~$27,588/year) + $2,000 onboarding fee
- **Positioning:** Mid-market influencer CRM
- **Features:**
  - Creator discovery & recruitment
  - Campaign management workflows
  - Content approvals
  - Payment processing
  - ROI tracking
- **Target:** Brands managing 20-40 creators/month
- **Gap:** Still expensive for agencies managing multiple small clients

#### **CreatorIQ**
- **Positioning:** Enterprise "operating system for creator-led growth"
- **Pricing:** **$35,000+/year** (typically $2,000-$2,500/mo minimum)
- **Target:** Global brands with massive creator programs
- **Features:**
  - Real-time API-powered discovery
  - Multi-stakeholder approval workflows
  - Advanced UGC/content rights management
  - Brand safety & compliance tools
  - Dedicated account management
- **Strength:** Most comprehensive enterprise solution
- **Gap:** **Massive overkill** for SMBs and even mid-size brands

### Brand Pain Points Managing Creators

Research identified these **top 4 pain points** (from poll of marketers):

1. **Generating Authentic Content (38.6%)**
   - Brands struggle to get creators to produce "on-brand but authentic" content
   - Overly scripted campaigns flop; too loose guidance = off-brand content

2. **Measuring ROI (36%)**
   - Hard to attribute sales/conversions to specific influencers
   - Multi-channel campaigns with multiple creators = attribution nightmare
   - Platforms lack unified conversion tracking

3. **Finding the Right Creators (16.1%)**
   - Discovery tools exist but require manual vetting
   - Fake followers epidemic (50%+ of influencers have inflated metrics)
   - Need: location, niche, engagement rate, audience demographics filters

4. **Managing Collaborations (9.3%)**
   - **Workflow chaos:** Email threads, spreadsheets, lost contracts
   - Slow approvals, last-minute changes, unanswered emails
   - 25% of creators cite ghosting/delays as routine issues
   - No centralized system to track: contracts, payments, content delivery, performance

**Additional Pain Points:**
- **Scaling challenges:** Manual outreach doesn't scale beyond ~20 creators
- **Product seeding logistics:** Fulfillment tracking, inventory management
- **Contract & payment management:** 14% cite this as major issue
- **Content rights:** Who owns what? Usage rights tracking is manual
- **Communication breakdown:** No unified inbox for creator conversations

**What Brands Are Currently Using (workarounds):**
- **Spreadsheets** — 80% of smaller brands still use Excel/Sheets
- **Email chains** — Disorganized, no audit trail
- **Multiple tools** — HubSpot for outreach + Slack for comms + manual payment tracking = friction

#### Gap 1: **Affordable Mid-Market Solutions**

- **Problem:** No unified view of 50 creators' performance across IG/TikTok/YouTube

- **Opportunity:** "Plausible for influencer marketing" — simple, live, beautiful analytics

- **Problem:** Tools are either "too simple" (Buffer) or "too complex" (CreatorIQ)

  - Automated outreach sequences (personalized)
  - Auto-reminders for content delivery
  - Simple approval workflows (mobile-friendly)
  - Payment scheduling & tracking

#### Gap 4: **Creator-First Tools**
- **Problem:** All tools are **brand-centric**. Creators hate using them.
- **Missing:** 
  - Creator self-service portal (pick products, submit content, track earnings)
  - Mobile-first creator experience
  - Instant payment options (vs. NET-30)
- **Opportunity:** Make creators **want** to use your platform (network effects)

#### Gap 5: **Micro/Nano Influencer Focus**
- **Problem:** Platforms optimize for mega-influencers (1M+ followers)
- **Reality:** Micro-influencers (10K-100K) have **better engagement & ROI**

- **Opportunity:** "ConvertKit for influencer marketing" — nurture 1,000 creators

#### Gap 6: **Content Licensing & Rights Management**
- **Problem:** Manual tracking of UGC usage rights
- **Missing:** Automated content library with license tracking, expiry alerts
- **Opportunity:** Integrate with brand asset management systems

#### Gap 7: **Vertical-Specific Solutions**
- **Problem:** Generic tools try to serve fashion, tech, beauty, food equally
- **Missing:** Industry-specific workflows (e.g., restaurant influencer seeding vs. SaaS demo creator campaigns)
- **Opportunity:** Vertical SaaS plays (e.g., "Grin for restaurants")

## 3. Technical Challenges

### Multi-Tenant Creator Analytics

**Challenge 1: Cross-Platform Data Aggregation**
- **Problem:** Each platform (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube) has different APIs, rate limits, and data schemas
- **Complexity:**
  - Instagram Graph API: Requires Business/Creator account OAuth
  - TikTok API: 1,000 requests/day limit, strict approval process
  - YouTube Data API: Per-channel permission needed for detailed metrics
  - Twitter (X) API: Expensive premium tiers for historical data
- **Solution Approaches:**
  - Use unified API services (Phyllo, Data365, Modash API)
  - Build custom scrapers (risky — TOS violations, IP bans)
  - Hybrid: Official APIs + scraping for public data

**Challenge 2: Real-Time vs Batch Processing**
- **Problem:** Brands want "live" dashboards; APIs have rate limits
- **Tradeoff:**
  - **Real-time:** Expensive, complex (WebSockets, polling), rate limit hell
  - **Batch (hourly/daily):** Cheaper, but data feels stale
- **Best Practice:** Hybrid approach
  - Real-time for critical metrics (campaign posts during launch)
  - Batch for historical trends

**Challenge 3: Dealing with Platform API Changes**
- **Problem:** Instagram deprecated Basic Display API; TikTok changes rules constantly
- **Impact:** Features break, need constant maintenance
- **Mitigation:** Abstract API layer, maintain fallbacks, subscribe to platform developer updates

**Challenge 4: Handling Deleted Content**
- **Problem:** Influencers delete posts; brands need records for ROI tracking
- **Solution:** Archive content snapshots (text, images, metrics at time of capture)
- **Storage Cost:** Can balloon with video content

**Challenge 5: Fake Metrics Detection**
- **Problem:** Bots, engagement pods, purchased followers
- **Technical Approach:**
  - Analyze follower/engagement ratios
  - Check follower authenticity (Modash does this)
  - Flag suspicious spikes in metrics
- **Challenge:** AI bots are getting better; detection is arms race

### API Rate Limits & Authentication

**Instagram Graph API:**
- **Rate Limit:** 200 calls/hour per user (official)
- **Auth:** Requires user OAuth (creator must grant permission)
- **Pain Point:** Can't bulk-scrape public data; limited to Business/Creator accounts
- **Workaround:** Apify Instagram Scraper (unofficial, risky)

**TikTok API:**
- **Rate Limit:** 1,000 requests/day (Research API)
- **Active User Cap:** Many devs report hitting undocumented user limits
- **Auth:** OAuth required; app approval process is slow
- **Pain Point:** "reached_active_user_cap" error — can't scale beyond ~100 creators
- **Workaround:** Use Modash/Phyllo aggregators who have higher limits

**YouTube Data API:**
- **Rate Limit:** 10,000 units/day (1 search = 100 units, 1 video details = 1 unit)
- **Auth:** API key for public data; OAuth for channel analytics
- **Pain Point:** Unit system is confusing; quotas can be exhausted quickly
- **Best Practice:** Cache aggressively, use batch requests

**Twitter (X) API:**
- **Rate Limit:** Free tier is extremely limited (50 tweets/15min)
- **Pricing:** Premium API is expensive ($5,000+/mo for serious usage)
- **Pain Point:** Elon-era API chaos; unpredictable changes

**Unified API Challenges:**
- **Latency:** Adding abstraction layer = slower response times
- **Cost:** Phyllo/Modash charge per-API-call or per-creator-tracked
- **Dependency Risk:** If Phyllo goes down or changes pricing, you're stuck

### Scale Considerations

**Database Architecture:**
- **Time-series data:** Creator metrics over time = massive DB growth
- **Solution:** Use InfluxDB, TimescaleDB, or cloud time-series services (AWS Timestream)

**Caching Strategy:**
- **Problem:** Can't hit APIs for every dashboard load
- **Solution:** Redis/Memcached for hot data (today's stats), refresh every 15-30min

**Multi-Tenancy:**
- **Challenge:** Isolate brand data; some brands manage same creators
- **Approach:** Row-level security, separate schemas per tenant, or full DB per tenant (expensive)

**Webhook Management:**
- **Problem:** Platforms send webhooks for post/comment events; need reliable queue
- **Solution:** SQS/RabbitMQ + idempotent processing (handle duplicates)

**Cost Scaling:**
- **API Costs:** Can balloon with usage (e.g., Modash $0.01 per search result × 10,000 searches/day = $100/day = $3K/mo)
- **Storage:** Video archiving is expensive (S3 costs add up)
- **Compute:** Real-time analytics = more server costs

## 4. Revenue Models

### Per-Seat Pricing
**Examples:** Sprout Social ($199-$399/seat/mo), Hootsuite, SocialPilot
- **Pros:**
  - Predictable revenue
  - Simple to understand
  - Scales with team size
- **Cons:**
  - Punishes collaboration (adds friction to adding users)
  - Doesn't align with value (managing 5 creators vs 500 creators = same price?)
  - Users share logins to avoid paying

**Typical Structure:**
- Base: $199/seat/mo (1-5 users)
- Volume discount: 10+ seats = 15% off

### Per-Creator Pricing
**Examples:** Some influencer platforms charge per "active creator" managed
- **Pricing:** $10-$50/creator/month
- **Pros:**
  - Aligns with value (more creators = more payment)
  - Scales naturally with customer growth
- **Cons:**
  - Can get expensive fast (100 creators = $5K/mo)
  - Brands may game system (only "activate" creators during campaigns)

**Potential Structure:**
- $20/creator/mo for active creators
- Bulk discounts: 50+ creators = $15/creator, 200+ = $10/creator

### Usage-Based Pricing
**Examples:** Modash API (credits), Phyllo (API calls), Zapier (tasks)
- **Metrics:**
  - API calls per month
  - Reports generated
  - Content pieces tracked
  - Data refreshes
- **Pros:**
  - Fairest alignment with value
  - Low barrier to entry
  - Scales revenue automatically
- **Cons:**
  - Unpredictable costs scare customers
  - Complex to communicate
  - Requires robust metering infrastructure

**Typical Structure:**
- 1,000 API calls = $10
- 10,000 tracked posts = $100/mo
- Unlimited users, pay for what you use

### Hybrid Models (Emerging Winner)
**Structure:** Base subscription + usage overage
- **Example:** $500/mo base (includes 25 creators + 10,000 API calls) + $15/creator overage + $0.01/extra API call
- **Pros:**
  - Predictable baseline revenue
  - Aligns with value at scale
  - Lets customers "grow into" higher tiers
- **Cons:**
  - More complex billing
  - Need sophisticated metering

### Freemium
**Example:** Buffer (free for 3 channels)
- **Strategy:** Free tier to hook users, upsell premium features
- **Influencer Platform Application:**
  - Free: Manage 5 creators, basic analytics
  - Pro: 50 creators, advanced analytics, integrations
  - Enterprise: Unlimited, API access, white-label
- **Conversion Rates:** Typically 2-5% free→paid

### Add-On Monetization
**Examples:** Sprout Social (social listening = extra $$$), Grin (extra modules)
- **Options:**
  - Advanced analytics: +$200/mo
  - Social listening: +$500/mo
  - API access: +$300/mo
  - White-label reports: +$150/mo
- **Pros:** Increases ARPU without forcing upgrades
- **Cons:** Can feel nickel-and-dime-y

### Services Layer
**Revenue Beyond Software:**
- **Managed services:** "We'll run your campaigns for you" (agency model)
- **Creator matchmaking:** Premium service to find perfect creators
- **Content production:** In-house studio for brand assets
- **Consulting:** Strategy workshops, training

**Pricing:** $5K-$50K/project or retainer

### Exploitable Gaps

#### Gap #1: The "$5K/Year Sweet Spot"
- **Problem:** Jump from $3K (social tools) to $20K (influencer tools) with nothing in between
- **Opportunity:** Build a **$400-$600/mo platform** with:
  - Manage 25-100 creators
  - Basic discovery (use Modash API)
  - Workflow automation (outreach, approvals, payments)
  - Cross-platform analytics dashboard
  - Self-serve onboarding (no sales call required)
- **Target:** Agencies managing 5-10 clients, each with 10-20 creators

#### Gap #2: Creator Collaboration Tools
- **Problem:** All tools are brand-centric; creators hate them
- **Opportunity:** Build **dual interface:**
  - **Brand side:** Campaign briefs, approvals, analytics
  - **Creator side:** Mobile app to claim campaigns, upload content, track earnings, instant payouts
- **Moat:** Network effects — creators want to join because payment is easy; brands want to join because creators are already there

#### Gap #3: Micro-Influencer CRM
- **Problem:** Tools optimize for managing 50 big creators, not 500 micro-creators
- **Opportunity:** "ConvertKit for influencers"
  - Segmentation & tagging
  - Automated email sequences
  - Relationship scoring (engagement, past performance)
  - Bulk actions (message 100 creators at once)
- **Target:** Brands going deep on micro-influencer strategy

#### Gap #4: Vertical-Specific Tools
- **Problem:** Generic tools can't capture industry nuances
- **Examples:**
  - **Restaurants:** Product = dine-in experience; need reservation booking integration
  - **SaaS:** Product = software demo; need trial signup tracking
  - **Beauty:** Product seeding + before/after content requirements
- **Opportunity:** Fork a horizontal tool into 3-5 verticals with custom workflows

#### Gap #5: Real-Time Campaign War Room
- **Problem:** Brands launch campaigns and check back 3 days later
- **Opportunity:** **Live campaign dashboard:**
  - WebSocket-powered real-time metrics
  - Alert system (post went viral, creator missed deadline)
  - Leaderboard of top-performing creators in campaign
- **Use Case:** Product launches, flash sales, event coverage

#### Gap #6: Content Licensing Marketplace
- **Problem:** UGC is valuable but rights management is chaos
- **Opportunity:** **Marketplace for licensed creator content:**
  - Creators upload content with usage terms (perpetual, 1-year, etc.)
  - Brands browse and purchase usage rights
  - Auto-contract generation
- **Revenue:** 15-30% take rate on transactions

#### Gap #7: Affiliate Network Integration
- **Problem:** Influencer platforms ≠ affiliate networks (ShareASale, Impact, CJ)
- **Opportunity:** Unified platform bridging both:
  - Track both brand deals AND affiliate sales
  - One creator profile with all revenue streams
- **Target:** Creators managing multiple income sources

## 6. Missing Angles & Adjacent Markets

### Opportunities Divy Might Not See

#### Angle #1: Creator Payroll/Fintech Play
- **Insight:** Payments are a **massive pain point** but platforms treat it as afterthought
- **Opportunity:** Become the **"Stripe for creator payments"**
  - Handle 1099s/tax forms automatically
  - Instant payouts (creators hate NET-30)
  - Multi-currency (global creators)
  - Split payments (co-created content)
- **Revenue:** 2-3% transaction fee = lucrative at scale
- **Precedent:** Gig economy platforms (Uber, DoorDash) control payments = control relationship

#### Angle #2: Creator Onboarding & Vetting as a Service
- **Insight:** Brands waste weeks vetting creators; most platforms just provide search
- **Opportunity:** **White-glove vetting service:**
  - Automated background checks (fake follower detection, past controversies)
  - Manual review by analysts for high-value partnerships
  - Deliver "creator dossier" with recommendation
- **Revenue:** $50-$200/creator vetted
- **Target:** Brands doing high-risk partnerships (pharma, finance)

#### Angle #3: Creator Learning & Certification
- **Insight:** Brands trust "certified" creators more
- **Opportunity:** **Platform-run certification program:**
  - "Instagram Shopping Expert" badge
  - "Brand Safety Certified"
  - Training courses on FTC disclosure, brand partnerships
- **Revenue:** $99-$499/course; brands pay premium for certified creators
- **Moat:** Two-sided value (creators upskill, brands get quality)

#### Angle #4: Insurance for Influencer Campaigns
- **Insight:** Campaigns fail (creator ghosting, content doesn't perform); brands eat loss
- **Opportunity:** **Campaign insurance product:**
  - Pay 10% premium; if creator doesn't deliver, get refund
  - Underwrite based on creator track record
- **Revenue:** Insurance margin (difference between premium and actual payout rate)
- **Precedent:** Event cancellation insurance

#### Angle #5: Creator Co-Ops & Collectives
- **Insight:** Individual creators have no leverage; brands have all power
- **Opportunity:** **Enable creator collectives:**
  - Group negotiation (10 creators bid as unit)
  - Shared contracts, shared revenue split
  - Platform facilitates formation & management
- **Revenue:** SaaS fee to collectives; maybe small take rate
- **Impact:** Disrupts power dynamic in favor of creators

#### Angle #6: AI-Powered "Virtual Influencers"
- **Insight:** CGI influencers (Lil Miquela) are growing; AI avatars getting better
- **Opportunity:** **Platform to create & manage virtual influencers:**
  - Brands create AI persona
  - Generate content at scale (AI images/videos)
  - Track engagement like real creators
- **Revenue:** SaaS + content generation credits
- **Risk:** Ethical concerns, audience backlash against "fake"

#### Angle #7: B2B Influencer Marketing (Missing Segment!)
- **Insight:** Everyone focuses on B2C (fashion, beauty); **B2B influencer marketing is untapped**
- **Examples:**
  - SaaS companies partnering with tech YouTubers
  - Enterprise software using LinkedIn thought leaders
  - Cybersecurity brands working with infosec Twitter accounts
- **Opportunity:** **LinkedIn-first influencer platform:**
  - Find B2B thought leaders
  - Track LinkedIn engagement, lead generation
  - Integrate with CRMs (HubSpot, Salesforce)
- **Pricing:** Higher budgets (B2B = $$$), less price-sensitive
- **Gap:** CreatorIQ/Grin barely touch this

### Adjacent Markets to Explore

#### 1. **UGC Content Agencies**
- Brands pay creators for content but manage campaigns in-house
- **Opportunity:** Software to run UGC agency operations (creator pool mgmt, content delivery, licensing)

#### 2. **Affiliate Marketing Platforms**
- Overlap with influencer marketing (creators driving sales)
- **Play:** Bridge gap between impact.com/ShareASale and influencer platforms

#### 3. **Employee Advocacy Tools**
- Make employees brand ambassadors (share on personal socials)
- **Examples:** GaggleAMP, EveryoneSocial
- **Opportunity:** Combine external influencers + internal employee advocates

#### 4. **Event/Experiential Marketing**

- **Gap:** No tools for managing creator events end-to-end
- **Opportunity:** "Eventbrite for creator events" with built-in tracking

#### 5. **PR & Media Outreach**
- Similar workflow to influencer outreach (pitching, tracking coverage)
- **Play:** Unified platform for influencer + journalist outreach

#### 6. **Talent Management (Creator Agencies)**
- Agencies representing creators need tools to manage clients
- **Opportunity:** "CRM for creator agencies" (earnings tracking, deal pipeline)

## Key Takeaways & Strategic Recommendations

### Where the Market Is Headed

1. **Consolidation:** Enterprise players will acquire smaller tools (e.g., Sprout acquiring Tagger)
2. **Hybrid Pricing:** Shift from per-seat to usage-based + subscriptions
3. **AI Integration:** Automated discovery, content generation, performance prediction
4. **Creator Empowerment:** Tools that serve creators (not just brands) will win via network effects
5. **Compliance Focus:** GDPR, FTC disclosure, brand safety = table stakes

### Immediate Opportunities (Next 12-24 Months)

1. **Build the "$500/mo influencer platform"** targeting agencies/SMBs
2. **Solve multi-creator analytics aggregation** with beautiful dashboard (Plausible-style)
3. **Creator payment infrastructure** (become Stripe of creator economy)
4. **Vertical SaaS play** (pick one: restaurants, SaaS, beauty)
5. **B2B influencer marketing** (LinkedIn-focused)

### Strategic Moats to Build

- **Data moat:** Proprietary creator performance data (who converts best)
- **Network effects:** Creators flock to platform = brands follow
- **Integration moat:** Deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration (Grin's strategy)
- **Workflow lock-in:** Custom workflows become mission-critical (hard to switch)
- **Payment lock-in:** Control money flow = control relationship

### Red Flags to Avoid

- **Don't compete head-on with CreatorIQ/Grin** in enterprise — they have moats
- **Don't build "yet another scheduling tool"** — saturated market
- **Don't ignore creator experience** — brand-only tools have limited moat
- **Don't underestimate API fragility** — platforms change rules overnight
- **Don't neglect compliance** — FTC, GDPR violations = existential risk
